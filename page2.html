Historical contexts

  • Parenti, Michael. Blackshirts and Reds: Rational Fascism and the Overthrow of Communism. City Lights Books, 1997.

Start with this! Very Accessible, fun short read. Goes over the development of fascism in Italy, Germany, the socialist movement, the legacy of the USSR, and the development and downfall of the Eastern Bloc. Written in 1997, after the end of the Cold War but before 9/11 - the absolute height of American hegemonic power. Even Russia was a US ally under Yeltsin at that point. Very interesting perspectives.

  • Green, John, and Brant de la Motte. Stasi State or Socialist Paradise? The German Democratic Republic and What Became of It. , 2015. If you want a pdf copy ask it is available on the internet.

After reading this you will want to revive Helmut Kohl just to kill him with your bare hands. And also kill the Federal Republic Of Germany.

Understanding Capitalism

  • Lenin, V. I. Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism. 1917. Marxists Internet Archive, www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1916/imp-hsc/.

Written in Switzerland during WWI, analyzing the economic roots of inter-imperialist rivalry. Chapters: 1. Concentration of Production and Monopolies; 2. Banks and Their New Role; 3. Finance Capital and the Financial Oligarchy; 4. Export of Capital; 5. Division of the World Among Capitalist Associations; 6. Division of the World Among the Great Powers; 7. Imperialism as a Special Stage of Capitalism; 8. Parasitism and Decay of Capitalism; 9. Critique of Imperialism; 10. The Place of Imperialism in History.

Praxis

  • Mao, Zedong, Oppose Book Worship. Selected Works of Mao Tse-Tung, 1930. Available via Marxists Internet Archive

Very short, accessible text, on applying marxist analysis, collecting data and understanding your own material conditions. Seriously, it takes like fifteen minutes to read, max. Dewit.

Propaganda pending.

  • Lenin, V. I. What Is To Be Done? Translated by Joe Fineberg and George Hanna, edited by Victor Jerome, Marxists Internet Archive, 1999, www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1901/witbd/.

Propaganda (not mine): A sharp polemic written in 1901–1902, arguing that workers focused only on economic struggles (wages, hours) will never achieve revolution. Lenin insists on a disciplined vanguard party of professional revolutionaries to bring class consciousness "from without." Written under tsarist censorship, the pamphlet crackles with urgency. Read alongside Parenti's Blackshirts and Reds, it provides the original blueprint for how a revolutionary party might attempt to seize and hold power in hostile conditions.

Burning Questions (re: Anarchism, Authority and the Vanguard Party)

  • Stalin, J. V. Anarchism or Socialism?. 1906-1907.  Marxists Internet Archive, www.marxists.org/reference/archive/stalin/works/1906/12/x01.htm.

A dense, combative pamphlet written in 1906–1907, when Stalin was a young Bolshevik in his late twenties.

  • Engels, Frederick, On Authority, 1872, Available on Marxists Internet Archive, https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1872/10/authority.htm 

A short piece on the ideas of anti-authoritarianism

  • Gaddafi, Muammar. The Green Book. 1975. Marxists Internet Archive, www.marxists.org/subject/africa/gaddafi/index.htm.

He contradicts some fundamental ideas that marxist-leninists take for granted. He ran a successful revolutionary state until NATO couped him in 2011 (Libya was the most developed country in Africa, believe it or not), so it’s worthwhile to take him seriously. He describes what I might call a mix between direct democracy and people’s democracy, and critiques the development of a nomenklatura within a marxist-leninist vanguard party.

  • Gelderloos, Peter, Anarchism Works. 2010. The Anarchist Library, https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/peter-gelderloos-anarchy-works 

Written by an Anarchist.

  • Lenin, V. I. The State and Revolution. 1918. Marxists Internet Archive, www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/.

A theoretical work written in hiding just months before the October 1917 Bolshevik seizure of power. Chapters: 1. Class Society and the State; 2. The Experience of 1848–51; 3. The Experience of the Paris Commune of 1871; 4. Continuation. Supplementary Explanations by Engels; 5. The Economic Basis of the Withering Away of the State; 6. The Vulgarisation of Marxism by the Opportunists; 7. The Experience of the Russian Revolutions of 1905 and 1917 (unwritten).
